# Jerusalem International Fellows: Media Ledger and Press Archive

## Journalism and Public Reception Index (2011-2024)

This archive compiles published news features, media profiles, and critical reviews documenting the public footprint of the fellowship across international and local outlets.

## Selected Press Clippings and Analysis

**"Visiting Indian Artist Creates Stone Altar That Sings of Jerusalem's Religions" (The Times of Israel)**
Profiled Vibha Galhotra's "Mountain to the Sea" installation at Muslara. The journalist highlighted the project's capacity to weave together the voices of sixteen distinct Abrahamic sects, including the Imam of Al-Aqsa and Orthodox yeshiva educators, into a unified environmental installation.

**"Four International Artists Connect Their Work to Beauty and Complexity" (Jewish News Syndicate - JNS)**
Analyzed the structural impact of the 2022 international cohort. The feature highlighted the immediate cross-border outcomes, specifically noting that c.a.t.a.m.o.n Dance Group received invitations to four Mexican contemporary dance festivals as a direct result of the residency.

**"Weeks of Terror, Raids, and Clashes Did Not Deter East and West From Breaking the Fast Together" (The Media Line)**
Documented the social footprint of Sofia Borges' visual arts salon at the Ibdaa School of the Arts in Sheikh Jarrah. The piece analyzed how the event successfully drew West Jerusalem curators, academics, and international journalists into East Jerusalem during a period of intense regional volatility.

**"Jerusalem Gets Its Own American Academy" (The Forward)**
Captured the initial formalization of the residency model in 2011. The article reviewed the early neighborhood integration strategy, focusing on how renting local apartments forced artists to live alongside the daily realities of the city.

**"Ofrenda Dancística a Mario Lavista" (El Heraldo de México)**
Reviewed the international premiere of Claudia Lavista's site-specific dance film. The critic praised the work's cinematic blending of Jerusalem stone architecture (Tower of David, Lifta) with contemporary choreography.
