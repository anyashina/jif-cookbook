Replication Manual: Fellow Recruitment and Selection Process

The Closed Nomination Engine

The program rejects open calls to protect applicant quality and cohort temperament. Operations rely on a warm network of expert nominators who understand the cultural friction of the host city.

The 18-Month Recruitment Lifecycle

  • Months 1-3 (Nominator Cultivation): The Executive Director engages a pool of curators, presenters, program alumni, and educators across specific disciplines. The team distributes crisp selection parameters during 1:1 orientation calls.

  • Months 4-6 (Nomination Window): Each nominator formally submits exactly two candidates, accompanying portfolios with clear written justifications.

  • Months 7-9 (Application Delivery): Selected nominees receive formal invitations to submit detailed packages, including project proposals adapted to the city context.

  • Months 10-12 (Vetting Pass): An independent panel conducts blind portfolio passes to evaluate professional excellence and collaborative aptitude, narrowing the pool to finalists.

  • Months 13-15 (Substantive Matchmaking): Finalists participate in facilitated video calls with host organization directors to confirm alignment, shared sensibilities, and space feasibility.

  • Month 16+ (Cohort Finalization): The Executive Director confirms the final cohort, optimizing for chemical balance across the group.

Dual-Pass Screening Criteria

  1. Pass 1: Professional Portfolio Vetting: Evaluates creative innovation, history of discipline delivery, and project feasibility within the 10-week block.

  2. Pass 2: Temperament Assessment: Screens for genuine humility, cross-cultural curiosity, and a willingness to work within intense environments. The engine filters out precious or isolationist mindsets.

Vetting Panel Decision Matrix

Panels evaluate finalists across four mandatory pillars:

  • Does this candidate align with the systemic mission of the fellowship? (Yes/No)
  • Does the artist display verified capacity to thrive in a small, intensive cohort? (Yes/No)
  • Does the proposed project scope build deep portals for community integration? (Yes/No)
  • Is the emergent relationship with the assigned host institution synergistic? (Synergistic/Neutral/Problematic)

A finalist requires three affirmative marks to advance. Any designation of a “Problematic” host relationship triggers immediate staff remediation before final cohort locking.
