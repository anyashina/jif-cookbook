# American Academy in Jerusalem: Plan for the Future (2014-2018 Strategic Document Summary)

## Institutional Context

The Foundation for Jewish Culture initiated a structural wind-down of its primary operations in 2014. To preserve the residency's latent potential, the executive leadership spent a month at the Rockefeller Foundation Bellagio Center drafting a comprehensive five-year expansion roadmap. The resulting document outlined a trajectory to scale the model into a multi-million-dollar independent entity.

## Strategic Scaling Targets

The plan proposed aggressive programmatic growth to expand the fellow footprint and maximize international soft power:

- **2014 Startup Baseline:** Maintained a 4-fellow cohort with an annual operating budget under $200,000.
- **2015-2016 Operational Scaling:** Projected expansion to 7 fellows in 2015 and 10 fellows by 2016, increasing annual spend to $865,000.
- **2017-2018 Dual-Cohort Model:** Planned the launch of two separate cohorts annually, hosting 8 fellows in 2017 and reaching 10 fellows by 2018. The total projected five-year expenditure totaled $4,492,000.

## Structural Rebranding and Nomenclature Insights

The Bellagio document explicitly identified a significant strategic hurdle: the original "American Academy in Jerusalem" brand caused persistent confusion within local communities and limited international fundraising. The plan mandated dropping the American branding constraint by 2017. Internationalizing the candidate pool allowed the program to welcome global participants, ultimately leading to the Jerusalem International Fellows nomenclature.

## Long-Term Funding Assumptions

The 2018 revenue model relied on a diversified pyramid structure:

- **Anchor Foundation Commitments:** Anticipated a $250,000 annual baseline from the Russell Berrie Foundation alongside sustained Bloomberg Philanthropies support.
- **Bilateral Institutional Revenue:** Assumed up to $750,000 in collaborative support from international embassies and local municipal culture budgets.
- **Philanthropic Cultivation:** Planned an annual gala network targeting $225,000 net, supplemented by individual fellow sponsorships and an endowment yield model.
