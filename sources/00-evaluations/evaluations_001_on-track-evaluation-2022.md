# 2022 Jerusalem International Fellows Pilot Residency: Independent Evaluation Report

## Executive Summary

The Jerusalem International Fellows invited leading international artists to participate in a unique ten-week residency program within the contemporary cultural landscape of East and West Jerusalem. The program matched four female fellows from Brazil, India, Mexico, and the United States with local institutional anchors. This structure facilitated deep creative collaborations with local artists, ensembles, and neighborhoods. The independent evaluation confirms that the pilot achieved resounding success and left a profound creative footprint across the city's cultural ecosystem.

## Core Program Elements

**Disorientation Week:** The residency initiated operations with a comprehensive, seven-day intensive tour of Jerusalem's historical, political, and religious layers. The protocol deliberately introduced conflicting narratives to challenge baseline preconceptions before fellows embedded with their hosts.

**Weekly Salons:** The team organized and executed over thirty intimate evening salons throughout the residency cycle. These gatherings brought together an average of fifteen to twenty-five invited artists, curators, and neighbors to engage directly with the fellows' emerging processes.

**Embedded Collaborations:** Each fellow advanced an independent, process-oriented project in tandem with their host institution. Notable outcomes included Vibha Galhotra's interfaith stone audio installation at Muslala and Claudia Lavista's site-specific dance film co-produced with c.a.t.a.m.o.n Dance Group.

## Participant Evaluations and Ratings

Participants evaluated operational categories on a scale from 0 to 5:

- **Arrival and Welcome Logistics:** 5.0 - The local team provided an exceptional reception and immediate acclimation support.
- **Disorientation Week Organization:** 4.75 - The tours offered comprehensive cultural depth, though participants flagged the schedule as overly fatiguing during a historic cold wave.
- **Shared Accommodations:** 2.0 - Housing all four fellows in a single Musrara apartment generated persistent friction and lacked adequate personal privacy.
- **Per Diem and Compensation:** 2.0 - The living stipend fell short of Jerusalem's high cost of living, inducing immediate sticker shock.

## Strategic Operational Recommendations

1. **Delineate Financial Responsibilities:** Outsource all bookkeeping, shekel-to-dollar transactions, and tax coding to a dedicated professional. The program director must focus exclusively on operational delivery rather than administrative financial hurdles.

2. **Professionalize Host Support:** Fund dedicated host organization coordinators rather than letting the host directors absorb daily operational burdens.

3. **Restructure Housing Models:** Secure separate neighborhood apartments housing a maximum of two fellows per unit to ensure professional boundaries and personal rest.

4. **Calibrate Calendar Timelines:** Explicitly forbid scheduling the residency during the overlap of Ramadan, Passover, and Easter. Holiday closures interrupt operational continuity and strain local staff capacity.
