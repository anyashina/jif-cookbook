Jerusalem International Fellows: Financial Operations Playbook

Version 1.0 | July 19, 2026

Status: Phase 1 (Structural) complete. Phase 2 (Reconciliation) built with verification flags for Elise data.

Prepared by: Alexandra Nyashina, ANyashina LLC
Prepared for: Elise Bernhardt, Jerusalem International Fellows

DOCUMENT ARCHITECTURE

This playbook captures the complete financial operations model for JIF replication. It is structured in two layers:

  • PHASE 1: Structural models and frameworks (rates, FTE calculations, cost templates) — these are stable across iterations
  • PHASE 2: Reconciliation and projections — flagged with what data is pending from Elise’s 2022 actuals

All projections are built from the 2026 Financial Model Framework (Kyiv/Krakow/Chicago templates). All actual figures are extracted from 2022 program records or flagged as needing verification.

PHASE 1: STRUCTURAL FINANCIAL MODELS

1. STAFFING COST MODEL: ROLES, RATES & FTE

Overview

JIF requires five core roles. All are essential; none can be absorbed into other positions without compromising program quality (per 2022 evaluation).

Role Definitions & Salary Ranges

Role 1: Executive Director (Founder/Advisor → Hired Director)

Purpose: Vision, strategy, board liaison, major donor cultivation, organizational sustainability

FTE: 1.0 (can split between part-time founder/advisor + full-time hired director as organization matures)

Hourly equivalent: Not typically hourly; salaried position

Annual salary ranges by cost-of-living context:

  • Kyiv (low COL): $30,000/year
  • Krakow (medium COL): $42,000/year
  • Chicago (high COL): $65,000/year

Notes:

  • This is the “Elise role” — historically held by founder
  • Salary reflects premium expertise in cultural diplomacy + fundraising
  • Can be split: founder as 0.5 FTE advisor + hired ED as 1.0 FTE salaried director
  • In startup phase (first 2 years), founder often unpaid or minimal stipend

Recommendation for replication:

  • Year 1: Founder/advisor 0.5 FTE (unpaid or $10K stipend) + hired ED 0.75 FTE at 60% of full salary
  • Year 2+: Full-time hired ED at full range for cost-of-living context

Role 2: Program Director (On-ground operations)

Purpose: Host org relationships, daily problem-solving, Fellow logistics, disorientation week design, salon coordination support

FTE: 1.0 (full-time, on-ground in program city)

Hourly rate (if part-time or contract): $30-45/hour depending on context

Annual salary ranges:

  • Kyiv: $28,000/year
  • Krakow: $38,000/year
  • Chicago: $58,000/year

Hiring timeline: 12-16 weeks before cohort arrival (critical per 2022 evaluation)

Requirements:

  • Deep local knowledge (neighborhoods, political landscape, cultural players)
  • Bilingual (local language + English minimum; trilingual preferred)
  • Relationship capital with host organizations
  • Project management + problem-solving under pressure
  • Can absorb minor logistics but should NOT do finance, communications, or HR alone

Notes from 2022:

  • Elana Ben-Haim served as PD (hire date: December 2021 or January 2022; verify with Elise)
  • 6-week compressed timeline meant Elana was hired very late
  • 2022 evaluation recommends: “Do not hire PD within 6 weeks of cohort start”
  • Salary to calibrate: PENDING Elise verification of Elana’s 2022 amount

Recommendation for replication:

  • Begin PD recruitment 16 weeks before cohort
  • Target local salary 20-30% above median (to attract experienced people away from other roles)
  • Budget for 2-week paid onboarding before cohort arrival

Role 3: Communications / PR / Salon Manager

Purpose: Guest invitations, translations, social media, salon coordination, media relations, event logistics

FTE: 1.0 (full-time, on-ground)

Hourly rate (if part-time): $25-40/hour depending on context

Annual salary ranges:

  • Kyiv: $18,000/year
  • Krakow: $26,000/year
  • Chicago: $40,000/year

Language requirements: Local language + English + ideally a third language (Arabic for Jerusalem; Spanish for Latin America, etc.)

Key responsibilities per 2022 evaluation:

  • Design + execute 3 dinners/week (36 total over 10 weeks) for up to 20 guests each
  • Manage invitations (curate local cultural figures to attend)
  • Translation: conversations, event materials, Fellow communication
  • Documentation: photos, notes, social media content
  • Coordination with host organizations on salon logistics
  • Ongoing guest relations + follow-up

2022 staffing:

  • Nermeen Shweiki held this role (Palestinian PR, translator, communications specialist)
  • Evaluation notes: “Full-time job on its own” — do not merge with PD role
  • Salary: PENDING Elise verification

Critical finding from 2022: This role was absolutely essential and could not be reduced without compromising salon quality. Estimate: 13-15 weeks of compressed work over 10-week residency due to weekly salon cycle.

Recommendation for replication:

  • Hire 8-10 weeks before cohort
  • Budget for 1-2 weeks onboarding on salon logistics + cultural landscape
  • Do not reduce FTE below 1.0 unless program scale drops to 2-3 Fellows

Role 4: Finance / Bookkeeper / Operations

Purpose: Budget management, receipt tracking, currency/payment processing, grant reporting, tax compliance

FTE: 0.5 (can be shared with other programs or external contractor)

Hourly rate: $35-55/hour depending on expertise required

Annual salary (if full-time):

  • Kyiv: $12,000/year (0.5 FTE = $6,000)
  • Krakow: $16,000/year (0.5 FTE = $8,000)
  • Chicago: $25,000/year (0.5 FTE = $12,500)

Requirements:

  • Local tax/accounting knowledge (critical)
  • Currency/international payment processing
  • Grant reporting compliance (foundation requirements)
  • Receipt organization + reconciliation
  • Budget tracking + variance analysis

2022 staffing:

  • This role was absorbed by Elise herself (founder doing finance = warning sign)
  • Evaluation recommendations: “Hire a separate Finance person. Do not ask Program Director to absorb this.”
  • Israeli/Palestinian bureaucracy + currency transfers are full-time burden

Critical finding: This is the most undersourced role. 2022 operated without formal ongoing accounting, which created budget blind spots.

Recommendation for replication:

  • Hire external accountant/bookkeeper 8-12 weeks before cohort
  • Can be 0.5 FTE shared with sister programs
  • Do NOT merge with Program Director role
  • Require: local tax knowledge + international payment processing

Role 5: Host Organization Coordinators (One per host org)

Purpose: On-site daily Fellow support, logistics, translation, problem-solving, host org liaison

FTE: 0.5-1.0 per host org (depends on host org size and Fellow needs)

Hourly rate: $18-35/hour depending on context

Annual amount per coordinator:

  • Kyiv: $4,000-6,000/year (typically part-time or shared with host org staff)
  • Krakow: $5,500-7,500/year
  • Chicago: $8,500-12,000/year

Number of coordinators:

  • 4 Fellows = 4 coordinators (minimum)
  • 6 Fellows = 6 coordinators
  • 8 Fellows = 8 coordinators

Total Host Org Coordinator budget:

  • 4 host orgs at $5,000 avg = $20,000/year (Kyiv example)
  • 6 host orgs at $6,500 avg = $39,000/year (Krakow example)
  • 8 host orgs at $10,000 avg = $80,000/year (Chicago example)

Staffing model:

  • Can be partially absorbed by host org (in-kind contribution)
  • Or hired/contracted by JIF program
  • Role exists only during active residency (10-12 weeks/year)

2022 staffing:

  • c.a.t.a.m.o.n: Elad Schechter (host org director, likely provided coordinator support in-kind)
  • Ibdaa: Melhem Bader (host org director, same)
  • Bloomfield Museum: Maya Halevy (host org director, same)
  • Muslala: Matan Israeli (host org director, same)
  • No separate coordinators hired; director + existing staff + artist handled logistics

2022 evaluation note: “Host org directors were stretched thin. Next iteration should hire/contract separate coordinators or fund host orgs to hire.”

Recommendation for replication:

  • Budget for dedicated coordinators (do not assume host org absorption)
  • If budget tight: fund host orgs to pay their own coordinators (in-kind credit model)
  • Coordinators hired 6-8 weeks before cohort start

3. SALON ECONOMICS: COST PER EVENT

Salon Structure

  • Frequency: 3 per week × 12 weeks = 36 total salons
  • Timing: Evening dinners (typically 6-8 PM)
  • Guests: 15-25 people average (mixture of host org staff, local artists, community figures, past Fellows)
  • Format: Hosted meal + informal conversation + Fellow project presentation

Cost Components per Salon

A. Food & Beverages

Options:

  1. Full catering (hired caterer): $12-18 per person × 20 people = $240-360 per salon
  2. Host org kitchen (using existing staff/facilities): $6-10 per person × 20 people = $120-200 per salon
  3. Potluck hybrid (program pays protein, guests bring sides): $8-12 per person × 20 people = $160-240 per salon

Beverage component (in addition to meal):

  • Wine/beer: $4-6 per person × 20 = $80-120
  • Coffee/tea/soft drinks: $2-3 per person × 20 = $40-60
  • Total beverages: $120-180 per salon

2022 model: Assumed host org kitchen + program-provided wine = $200-250 per salon

Per-salon food + beverage cost range:

  • Low: $160 (potluck + budget beverages)
  • Mid: $250 (host org kitchen + good beverages)
  • High: $480 (full catering + full bar)

B. Translation

Scenario 1: Communications Manager is bilingual/trilingual, no external cost

  • Cost: $0 (absorbed into salary)

Scenario 2: Need freelance translator for specific languages

  • Rate: $30-50/hour depending on language/context
  • Duration per salon: 2-3 hours (setup, event, cleanup)
  • Cost: $60-150 per salon

2022 model: Nermeen Shweiki provided translation; no external cost

  • Implicit cost: embedded in her salary for this work

C. Documentation (Photography/Video)

Scenario 1: Communications Manager takes photos/videos (smartphone)

  • Cost: $0 (time already paid)

Scenario 2: Professional photographer for key salons (e.g., final salons)

  • Rate: $40-60/hour
  • Duration: 2-3 hours per event
  • Cost: $80-180 per salon (only for select events)

2022 model: In-house documentation (staff photos)

  • Estimated cost: $0-20 per event (media card, storage)

D. Space Rental (If Not at Host Org)

Scenario 1: Host organization provides space (free)

  • Cost: $0 (in-kind)

Scenario 2: Neutral venue (cafe, gallery, rental space)

  • Cost: $50-150 per salon depending on city

2022 model: Salons held at host organizations + one at FeelBeit (neutral space, free partner space)

  • Estimated cost: $0-50 per salon

E. Invitations & Communications

Cost components:

  • Email invitations: $0 (free platforms)
  • Printed invitations (select events): $0.50-1.00 per card × 25 cards = $12-25
  • Social media promotion: $0 (staff time)
  • Phone/WhatsApp reminders: $0-10 (if international messaging)

Estimated cost: $20-30 per salon

Week -16 to -8 (Pre-Cohort, Hiring Phase)

  • Program Director hiring: $28,000-58,000 (lump salary or begin monthly)
  • Coordinator hiring: $5,000-40,000 (depends on scale and FTE model)
  • Insurance policy: $4,000-12,000 (annual, upfront)
  • Rental deposit for housing: $1,000-3,000 (refundable security)

Estimated outflow: $38,000-113,000 (primarily staffing + insurance)

KEY CASH FLOW MANAGEMENT PRINCIPLES

1. Staging: Spread staffing costs across hiring window (don’t hire everyone in Week -8) 2. Reserve: Keep 10% contingency available for unplanned costs (illness, weather, security issues) 3. Payroll cycles: Establish consistent payment schedule (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly) early 4. Currency: If multiple currencies involved, lock exchange rates or use hedging 5. Reimbursements: Track all Fellow expenses (airfare, transport) for reimbursement verification
