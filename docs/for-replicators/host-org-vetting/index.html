Replication Manual: Host Organization Vetting and Partnership Rubric

Partnership Principles

Host organizations function as institutional co-creators and local anchors rather than passive venue providers. Replicators must select hosts that represent the diverse demographic and geographic realities of the city.

Evaluation Rubric: Six Core Criteria

Vetting teams must evaluate prospective hosts utilizing a formal six-part pass framework:

  1. Portal Capacity: The institution demonstrates verified expertise and active networks within the fellow’s artistic discipline.

  2. Leadership Commitment: The director commits personal time to participate in strategic planning meetings and weekly salon gatherings.

  3. Staff Bandwidth: The host allocates internal resources to support daily integration without letting the fellow work in isolation.

  4. Navigational Literacy: The organization safely operates across local cultural or political divides and moves across neighborhoods effectively.

  5. Ecosystem Fit: The anchor provides direct entry points to a vibrant network of young local creatives and independent practitioners.

  6. Financial Stability: The host possesses baseline organizational stability and agrees to use the stipend strictly for project-specific outlays rather than facility overhead.

Disqualifying Warning Signs

  • Fiscal Parasitism: The institution seeks partnership primarily to subsidize legacy deficits, utility bills, or unrelated staff salaries.

  • Complexity Avoidance: The leadership operates within a single cultural bubble and refuses to engage with surrounding neighborhoods or alternative viewpoints.

  • Territorial Silos: The organization isolates its participants and displays protective or exclusionary attitudes toward neighboring institutions.

  • Staff Ambivalence: The director signs the agreement without securing internal alignment, leading to operational neglect once the fellow arrives.

The 5-Month Cultivation Lifecycle

  • Month 1 (Discovery): Conduct initial introductory meetings with leadership to outline program philosophy and listen for institutional hesitation.

  • Month 2 (Site Vetting): Execute physical site visits to verify workspace parameters, event facilities, and transit accessibility.

  • Month 3 (Role Definition): Facilitate structured scoping sessions to establish shared collaborative outcomes and identify the part-time coordinator anchor.

  • Month 4 (MOU Finalization): Execute a simple, one-page partnership agreement defining stipends, resource boundaries, and clear exit clauses.

  • Month 5+ (Pre-Arrival Embedding): Integrate the host into virtual incubation calls with selected fellows to lock in project scopes.
