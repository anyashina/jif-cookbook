# Replication Manual: The Methodology of Serendipity

## Core Philosophy

The Jerusalem International Fellows framework functions as an active intervention in social and political deadlocks rather than a traditional cloistered artist retreat. The model uses the unique saturations of divided urban geographies to inspire contemporary creative practice. By embedding international creators within local institutional anchors, the program establishes portals of engagement that bypass polarized external narratives.

## The Five Guiding Questions for City Adaptation

Replication teams must evaluate their local context by answering five structural questions:

1. **What is the specific unresolved tension or stereotype in your city?** The program addresses specific, lived frictions (such as economic segregation or immigration borders) where multiple communities claim the same space under competing assumptions.

2. **Who are the cultural leaders who have earned trust on both sides of that tension?** Replicators must spend eighteen to twenty-four months cultivating relationships with local grassroots leaders who possess verified credibility across community divides.

3. **How do you build housing, salon spaces, and host structures around that tension?** The geography must be intentional. House fellows near the seam lines and distribute salons across divided neighborhoods to force diverse audiences to gather.

4. **How do you select artists who can actively engage with the tension?** The selection engine targets mid-career creators demonstrating verified group flexibility and a process-oriented social practice rather than artists seeking isolated studio retreats.

5. **What does a ten-week timeline look like in your specific season?** Ten weeks provides the optimal duration for authentic relationships to deepen and survive initial misunderstandings without overtaxing host capacity.

## Non-Negotiable Operational Parameters

**Structured Disorientation:** Week 1 must expose fellows to competing local narratives through curated expert tours, deliberately interrupting baseline assumptions before host placement.

**Intimate Salon Architecture:** Salons must remain small (fifteen to twenty-five invited guests) and center on shared meals and work-in-progress exchanges rather than public exhibitions.

**Unpredictable Outcomes:** Replicators control the operational conditions and structure but must never dictate or predetermine the final artistic output.
