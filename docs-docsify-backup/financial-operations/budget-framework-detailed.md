# Budget and Funding Framework

The program operates on a per-cohort basis. Cost models vary significantly by city, cohort size, and local cost of living. The 2022 program cost structure is documented below as a reference point; salary ranges, housing costs, and stipends will change by location and year.

## Direct Fellowship Costs

### Per-Fellow Cost Breakdown (2022 Jerusalem Reference Model)

For a 10-week international residency, budget the following per Fellow:

**Airfare (round-trip, international):** $2,000
**Ground transportation** (airport pickup, local taxis, tours): $300
**Housing** (12 weeks, shared apartment, utilities included): $7,500
**Artist stipend, per diem, and materials:** $8,500
**Insurance** (international travel and residency coverage): $500

**Base Subtotal per Fellow: $19,000**

**Cost-of-living adjustment for Jerusalem 2022:** +$1,300 (based on evaluation feedback that per diem was insufficient)

**Total per Fellow: $20,300**

For a 4-Fellow cohort: **$81,200 total**
For a 5-Fellow cohort: **$101,500 total**
For a 6-Fellow cohort: **$121,800 total**

### Critical Note on Cost Variation by City

This Jerusalem 2022 model is a reference only. Each replication city requires recalibration:

**Low Cost-of-Living Example (Kyiv baseline):**
- Per-Fellow total: $6,710
- Significantly lower housing ($1,512 for 12 weeks) and meals ($1,008 for 10 weeks)
- Airfare similar; ground transport lower

**Medium Cost-of-Living Example (Krakow baseline):**
- Per-Fellow total: $9,912
- Housing: $2,940; Meals: $1,512
- Mid-range between Kyiv and Chicago

**High Cost-of-Living Example (Chicago baseline):**
- Per-Fellow total: $16,032
- Housing: $4,320 (12 weeks); Meals: $4,112
- Airfare lower than international residencies; catering higher

**For your replication city:** Use local cost-of-living indices. Key variables are housing (typically the largest single cost), meals, and local transportation. Ensure artists do not experience "sticker shock" and are adequately compensated for 10 weeks of forgone income.

### Evaluation Finding on Artist Stipends

The 2022 evaluation noted: "Per diem was insufficient for Jerusalem's cost of living. Artists experienced sticker shock. Artists felt strongly about need for honorarium/stipend to compensate for forgone income during residency."

Recommendation: Budget conservatively and increase rather than cut. Artists making $40,000-$80,000 annually in their home countries are sacrificing significant income. The residency stipend should reflect this opportunity cost.

## Group Infrastructure Costs

Beyond individual Fellow costs, the program requires investment in group activities, salons, and infrastructure. Reference model based on 2022 for a 4-Fellow cohort:

### Disorientation Week

**Professional guides** (2-3 guides with cultural credibility, 5 days): $1,200
**Transportation** (buses, shuttles, daily movements): $400
**Meals** (breakfast, lunch, dinner for 4 Fellows + staff, 5 days): $600
**Site entry fees** (museums, religious sites, visitor passes): $200

**Disorientation Week Subtotal: $2,400**

Timing recommendation: Schedule to avoid major holidays. The 2022 program coincided with Passover, Easter, and Ramadan, creating closures and scheduling confusion.

### Weekly Salons (36 events over 10 weeks, 3 per week)

Salons are the connective tissue of the program. Each requires curation, translation, refreshments, documentation, and facilitation.

**Per-Salon Labor:** One salon requires approximately 8-12 hours of work for the Communications Manager (planning, invitation curation, translation preparation, setup, facilitation, cleanup). Three salons per week = 24-36 hours of weekly salon work alone.

**Per-Salon Cost Breakdown:**

Refreshments (wine, cheese, bread, dessert for 15-25 people): $160-$480
Translation support and bilingual coordination: $50-$150
Documentation (photographer/videographer embedded or event-specific): $0-$180
Space rental (some at host orgs, some rented venues): $0-$150

**Cost per salon (range): $210-$960, average approximately $500**

**For 36 salons annually: $7,560-$34,560**

2022 actual allocation: Approximately $9,000 (36 salons × $250 average)

### Host Organization Stipends

Each host organization receives support to cover additional staffing, space rental, and operational costs created by hosting a Fellow.

**Per-organization stipend (reference 2022): $1,500-$2,000**

For 4 host organizations: **$6,000-$8,000**

This covers:
- Additional staff time for coordination and support
- Materials and supplies
- Space modifications or rentals
- Translation and communications
- Emergency/contingency support

The 2022 evaluation noted: "All the host organizations (barring the museum) felt they fell short of important resources to best support the artists. Solutions were found for most problems." Recommendation: Increase host stipends in future years if possible. The stipend should reflect actual organizational need, not just a standard formula.

### Documentation and Archival

**Documentary filmmaker/videographer** (embedded during 10-week residency): $4,000-$5,000
**Video editing and post-production:** $2,000-$3,000
**Photography** (salon documentation, Fellow work, archival): $1,500-$2,000
**Photo editing and archival:** $500-$1,000
**Salon recordings and transcription** (with consent): $500-$1,500

**Documentation Subtotal: $8,500-$12,500**

The 2022 evaluation recommends independent evaluation within 6-8 weeks of program end. Budget an additional $2,000-$3,000 for external evaluator fees.

### Group Infrastructure Total

**Disorientation week:** $2,400
**Salons (36 events):** $9,000
**Host organization stipends:** $7,500
**Documentation:** $8,500
**Contingency for group activities:** $2,000

**Total Group Infrastructure: $29,400**

This represents approximately 20-25% of total program budget and is the investment in shared experience and connective infrastructure.

## Funding Strategy and Diversification

The program requires diversified funding to be sustainable. Concentration in a single funder creates vulnerability.

### Recommended Funding Mix

**Major Foundation Grants** (Russell Berrie Foundation as model): 30-40% of budget
- Example: $50,000 for $150,000 program
- Typically multi-year commitments with reporting requirements
- Cultivation timeline: 18-24 months

**Mid-Level Foundation Grants** (Bloomberg, Jerusalem Foundation, city-specific equivalents): 20-30% of budget
- Example: $30,000-$45,000 for $150,000 program
- Often one-year grants with specific programmatic requirements
- Cultivation timeline: 12-18 months

**Individual Sponsorships** (Fellow-based, general arts support): 20-30% of budget
- Example: $30,000-$45,000 for $150,000 program
- Can be tied to specific Fellow or general program support
- Cultivation timeline: Ongoing, 6-12 months for major donors

**In-Kind Support** (host organizations, translators, documentarians, space): 10-15% of budget
- Example: $15,000-$22,500 in-kind value for $150,000 program
- Leverages local institutional relationships
- Not cash, but reduces actual cash requirements

## Contingency Planning and Budget Reserves

### Contingency Reserve Allocation

Maintain a contingency reserve of 10% of annual budget for unexpected costs. This reserve covers:

- Emergency housing changes or relocations
- Unexpected insurance or medical costs
- Host organization emergency needs
- Currency fluctuations (in international contexts)
- Mid-program staffing changes or contingencies
- Equipment or technology failures
- Political or security situation adjustments

**2022 Example:** Contingency funds were used when Claudia Lavista needed insurance to film at Ein Prat nature preserve, enabling that project's completion.

### Budget Flexibility Framework

Build into every budget 3-week flexibility in program dates. If political situation becomes unsafe in October, the program can delay to mid-October or January. If host organization has emergency, the program can pause and resume with minimal disruption.

### Scaling Down: What is Essential, What Can Be Cut

If funding falls short and you cannot meet the full budget, this is the priority order (do not cut below this line):

1. **Salaries** (you cannot operate without staff on the ground; this is non-negotiable)
2. **Housing and airfare** (Fellows cannot participate without basic support)
3. **Host organization stipends** (reducing these breaks partnerships)
4. **Salon program** (this is the core methodology; reduce from 36 to 24 salons, not zero)

**Acceptable cuts if necessary:**
- Documentation scope (reduce to essential video/photo, no embedded videographer)
- US administrative (reduce founder/advisor time allocation)
- Contingency reserve (cut to 5% instead of 10%)

**Never cut:**
- Program Director or Communications Manager positions
- Disorientation week (essential to methodology)
- Host organization partnerships

If the program cannot cover all core costs, better to run at smaller scale (4 Fellows instead of 5-6) than to run a full cohort with inadequate support.

## Financial Reporting and Accountability

### Documentation Required During Residency

- Weekly budget tracking (actual spend vs. budget)
- Receipt reconciliation (all expenses documented with receipts and coding)
- Currency transfer documentation (for international programs)
- Payroll and staff time tracking
- Salon and event cost tracking

### Documentation Required Post-Residency

- Final P&L (revenue and expenses with full reconciliation)
- Financial report to each major funder (per grant requirements, typically within 60 days of program end)
- Independent evaluation of program impact (6-8 weeks after program end)
- Archival of financial records (receipts, invoices, communications) for 3+ years

### Next Director Onboarding

The incoming director should receive:
- Complete 2022 P&L with actual figures (not reconstruction)
- Donor list with 2022 contribution amounts
- Staff salary documentation and justification
- Three-year funding forecast (2024, 2025, 2026)
- Fundraising strategy and prospect list
- Timeline for grant applications (12-24 months in advance)
